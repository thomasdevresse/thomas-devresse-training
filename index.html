The Kaizen path

Progress is a system, not a burst of motivation.

The goal is not to keep you dependent on coaching. It is to make each decision clearer until you can own the process.

Kaizen means making the next useful improvement without rushing faster than you can adapt. Coaching moves through four stages—and what each cycle teaches feeds the next one, so the steps compound.

01

Assess

Map your starting point, movement capacity, habits, constraints and objective.

02

Build

Strengthen the foundations and create a program your body can progress through.

03

Consolidate

Review the data, adjust training and habits, and make progress repeatable.

04

Own it

Develop the judgement and skills to keep progressing with less supervision.

What you learn feeds the next assessment—the cycle restarts, one level up.

A mutual standard

Coaching works when both sides do the work.

This is for you if…

  • You are ready to practise consistently, not only when motivation is high.
  • You accept progressive difficulty and honest feedback.
  • You want to understand the process—not just follow instructions.
  • You will apply the plan between sessions.

This is not the right fit if…

  • You want a quick fix or a passive weekly workout.
  • You are unwilling to change anything outside the session.
  • You need medical rehabilitation or treatment.
  • You want a guarantee without committing to the process.
