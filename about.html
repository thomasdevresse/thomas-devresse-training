About Thomas

I coach the way I train: observe, test, refine.

Calm does not mean comfortable. My role is to create clarity, demand honest effort and teach you how to make better training decisions over time.

The standard

Coaching should create capability, not dependence.

I do not see personal training as counting repetitions or providing motivation for one hour. Good coaching identifies what is limiting progress, chooses the right intervention and explains enough for the client to take ownership.

That is why every collaboration begins with assessment. Mobility, motor control, movement patterns, strength, training history and real-life constraints all affect the plan. The program then evolves through observation, feedback and reassessment.

Kaizen gives that process its rhythm: small improvements, repeated honestly, until they become a different standard.

From education to practice

Qualifications matter only when they improve the coaching decision.

See the limiting factor

Assessment helps distinguish a mobility restriction from a control problem, a technical issue or a capacity that simply needs to be trained.

Choose the useful intervention

Program design turns that information into exercise selection, loading and progressions that match the person rather than a generic template.

Review without guessing

Movement, strength, habits and progress markers are reassessed so the plan changes when the evidence changes.

Teach ownership

The aim is not permanent dependence. Clear explanations and progressive responsibility build better decisions beyond the coached session.
